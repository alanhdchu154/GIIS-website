## Big Ideas (spiraled across all units)

- **CHA — Change**: "How is calculus the mathematics of change?"
- **LIM — Limits**: "How do limits allow us to model and solve problems involving instantaneous rates and accumulated quantities?"
- **FUN — Analysis of Functions**: "How do we use derivatives and integrals to study the behavior of functions?"

## Unit 1 — Limits and Continuity

**Exam weighting**: 10–12% MC · ~22–23 class periods.
**Pedagogical role**: Foundation — limits introduce instantaneous behavior, the conceptual core of derivatives and integrals.

Topics (with CB topic codes):

- **CHA 1.1** — Introducing Calculus: Can Change Occur at an Instant?
- **LIM 1.2** — Defining Limits and Using Limit Notation
- **LIM 1.3** — Estimating Limit Values from Graphs
- **LIM 1.4** — Estimating Limit Values from Tables
- **LIM 1.5** — Determining Limits Using Algebraic Properties of Limits
- **LIM 1.6** — Determining Limits Using Algebraic Manipulation
- **LIM 1.7** — Selecting Procedures for Determining Limits
- **LIM 1.8** — Determining Limits Using the Squeeze Theorem
- **LIM 1.9** — Connecting Multiple Representations of Limits
- **LIM 1.10** — Exploring Types of Discontinuities
- **LIM 1.11** — Defining Continuity at a Point
- **LIM 1.12** — Confirming Continuity over an Interval
- **LIM 1.13** — Removing Discontinuities
- **LIM 1.14** — Connecting Infinite Limits and Vertical Asymptotes
- **LIM 1.15** — Connecting Limits at Infinity and Horizontal Asymptotes
- **FUN 1.16** — Working with the Intermediate Value Theorem (IVT)

**Common AP traps**:
- Confusing limit existence with function value (`lim x→a f(x) = L` doesn't require `f(a) = L`)
- Forgetting one-sided limits when evaluating limits at jump discontinuities
- Misapplying limit laws when factors are not finite/nonzero

## Unit 2 — Differentiation: Definition and Fundamental Properties

**Exam weighting**: 10–12% MC · ~13–14 class periods.
**Pedagogical role**: Translates the limit-based instantaneous rate concept into the algebraic machinery of derivatives.

- **CHA 2.1** — Defining Average and Instantaneous Rates of Change at a Point
- **CHA 2.2** — Defining the Derivative of a Function and Using Derivative Notation
- **CHA 2.3** — Estimating Derivatives of a Function at a Point
- **FUN 2.4** — Connecting Differentiability and Continuity
- **FUN 2.5** — Applying the Power Rule
- **FUN 2.6** — Derivative Rules: Constant, Sum, Difference, and Constant Multiple
- **FUN 2.7** — Derivatives of `cos x`, `sin x`, `eˣ`, and `ln x`
- **FUN 2.8** — The Product Rule
- **FUN 2.9** — The Quotient Rule
- **FUN 2.10** — Finding the Derivatives of Tangent, Cotangent, Secant, and/or Cosecant Functions

**Common AP traps**:
- Memorizing rules without conceptual grounding in the limit definition
- Misapplying product/quotient rule with wrong sign conventions
- Forgetting that differentiability implies continuity (not the reverse)

## Unit 3 — Differentiation: Composite, Implicit, and Inverse Functions

**Exam weighting**: 9–13% MC · ~10–11 class periods.

- **FUN 3.1** — The Chain Rule
- **FUN 3.2** — Implicit Differentiation
- **FUN 3.3** — Differentiating Inverse Functions
- **FUN 3.4** — Differentiating Inverse Trigonometric Functions
- **FUN 3.5** — Selecting Procedures for Calculating Derivatives
- **FUN 3.6** — Calculating Higher-Order Derivatives

**Common AP traps**:
- Forgetting to apply the chain rule (especially for nested or composite functions)
- Sign errors in implicit differentiation when isolating `dy/dx`
- Mixing up `arcsin(x)` and `(sin x)⁻¹` notation

**Exam weighting**: 10–15% MC · ~10–11 class periods.

- **CHA 4.1** — Interpreting the Meaning of the Derivative in Context
- **CHA 4.2** — Straight-Line Motion: Connecting Position, Velocity, and Acceleration
- **CHA 4.3** — Rates of Change in Applied Contexts Other Than Motion
- **CHA 4.4** — Introduction to Related Rates

- **CHA 4.6** — Approximating Values of a Function Using Local Linearity and Linearization
- **LIM 4.7** — Using L'Hospital's Rule for Determining Limits of Indeterminate Forms

**Common AP traps**:
- Forgetting units in real-world rate problems
- Setting up related rates with wrong variable relationships
- Misapplying L'Hospital's Rule outside indeterminate forms (0/0 or ∞/∞)

## Unit 5 — Analytical Applications of Differentiation

**Exam weighting**: 15–18% MC · ~15–16 class periods. **Highest-weighted AB unit.**

- **FUN 5.1** — Using the Mean Value Theorem
- **FUN 5.2** — Extreme Value Theorem, Global Versus Local Extrema, and Critical Points
- **FUN 5.3** — Determining Intervals on Which a Function Is Increasing or Decreasing
- **FUN 5.4** — Using the First Derivative Test to Determine Relative (Local) Extrema
- **FUN 5.5** — Using the Candidates Test to Determine Absolute (Global) Extrema
- **FUN 5.6** — Determining Concavity of Functions over Their Domains
- **FUN 5.7** — Using the Second Derivative Test to Determine Extrema
- **FUN 5.8** — Sketching Graphs of Functions and Their Derivatives
- **FUN 5.9** — Connecting a Function, Its First Derivative, and Its Second Derivative
- **FUN 5.10** — Introduction to Optimization Problems
- **FUN 5.11** — Solving Optimization Problems
- **FUN 5.12** — Exploring Behaviors of Implicit Relations

**Common AP traps**:
- Confusing critical points with extrema (every extremum is a CP, not vice versa)
- Mean Value Theorem hypotheses (continuous on `[a,b]` AND differentiable on `(a,b)`)
- Optimization: forgetting to verify max vs min via second derivative or boundary check

## Unit 6 — Integration and Accumulation of Change

**Exam weighting**: 17–20% MC · ~18–20 class periods. **Highest-weighted unit overall.**

- **CHA 6.1** — Exploring Accumulations of Change
- **LIM 6.2** — Approximating Areas with Riemann Sums
- **LIM 6.3** — Riemann Sums, Summation Notation, and Definite Integral Notation
- **FUN 6.4** — The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus and Accumulation Functions
- **FUN 6.5** — Interpreting the Behavior of Accumulation Functions Involving Area
- **FUN 6.6** — Applying Properties of Definite Integrals
- **FUN 6.7** — The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus and Definite Integrals
- **FUN 6.8** — Finding Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals: Basic Rules and Notation
- **FUN 6.9** — Integrating Using Substitution
- **FUN 6.10** — Integrating Functions Using Long Division and Completing the Square
- **FUN 6.14** — Selecting Techniques for Antidifferentiation

(BC-only topics — FUN 6.11 Integration by Parts, FUN 6.12 Linear Partial Fractions, LIM 6.13 Improper Integrals — excluded)

**Common AP traps**:
- Sign errors when computing `∫ from a to b` vs `∫ from b to a`
- Forgetting `+C` on indefinite integrals
- Confusing accumulation function `F(x) = ∫ from a to x f(t)dt` derivative with `f(x)` (FTC)
- Substitution: forgetting to change `dx` to `du` and update bounds

## Exam Structure (informational, not lesson content)

- **Section I — Multiple Choice**: 45 questions, 105 minutes, 50% of score
  - Part A (60 min, 30 questions, NO calculator)
  - Part B (45 min, 15 questions, graphing calculator required)
- **Section II — Free Response**: 6 questions, 90 minutes, 50% of score
  - Part A (30 min, 2 questions, graphing calculator required)
  - Part B (60 min, 4 questions, NO calculator)
