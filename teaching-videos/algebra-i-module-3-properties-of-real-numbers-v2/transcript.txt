Welcome back. Algebra One, Module Three — Properties of Real Numbers. These five properties are the rules that let you rearrange, regroup, and rewrite expressions without breaking the math. Every line you simplify from here on uses one of them.

Add seventeen plus twenty-five plus thirteen. In order it is awkward. But seventeen plus thirteen is thirty, plus twenty-five is fifty-five — done in your head. You did not change the answer. You used a property. That move is what this lesson teaches you to name.

Game plan. First, the five properties — commutative, associative, distributive, identity, and inverse. Second, the trap that costs the most points — they do not work on subtraction or division the same way. Third, a worked simplification where every step is labeled with the property used.

Commutative — you can swap the order. Addition: a plus b equals b plus a. Multiplication: a times b equals b times a. Subtraction and division do not commute — five minus two is not two minus five. Order is free for plus and times, never for minus and divide.

Associative — you can regroup. Parentheses move around without changing the answer. The quantity a plus b, plus c, equals a plus the quantity b plus c. Same for multiplication. Subtraction and division do not associate — three minus the quantity two minus one is two, but three minus two minus one is zero.

Distributive — the workhorse. a times the quantity b plus c equals a times b plus a times c. It is the only property that mixes multiplication and addition. Three times the quantity x plus four equals three x plus twelve. You will use this on almost every simplification this year.

Identity and inverse. Add zero to anything, you get it back — zero is the additive identity. Multiply by one, same thing — one is the multiplicative identity. The additive inverse of a is negative a — they sum to zero. The multiplicative inverse of a is one over a — they multiply to one.

Biggest trap — assuming every operation behaves like addition. It does not. Subtraction is not commutative, division is not associative, and the distributive property only links times with plus. Memorize the pairing — commutative and associative belong to plus and times. Minus and divide are not in that club.

Simplify three times the quantity x plus four, plus two x, minus five. First, distribute three across the parentheses. Then rearrange and regroup using commutative and associative. Then combine like terms. Final answer — five x plus seven. Every step is one named property.

Time. Distribute — two x plus ten, plus three x minus three. Commutative — rearrange as two x plus three x, plus ten minus three. Associative — group the x terms and the constants. Combine — five x plus seven. Same final form as the worked example, by design.

Recap. Commutative swaps order. Associative regroups parentheses. Distributive spreads a multiplication across a sum. Identity adds zero or multiplies by one. Inverse pairs a number with its opposite or its reciprocal. None of the swap-or-regroup properties work on subtraction or division.
