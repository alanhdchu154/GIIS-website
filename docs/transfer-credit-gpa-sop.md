# GIIS Transfer Credit and GPA Conversion SOP

Version: 1.0  
Effective date: 2026-07-21  
Owner: President & Principal / Admissions  
Applies to: Transfer applicants and currently enrolled GIIS students requesting prior-credit review  
Status: Internal operating policy. Parent-facing summaries may quote the approved language in Appendix D.

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines how Genesis of Ideas International School (GIIS) reviews prior coursework, accepts transfer credits, converts grades, calculates GPA impact, documents decisions, and explains outcomes to families.

The goal is to be fair to transfer students without weakening the credibility of GIIS transcripts. Prior work can help a student move faster, but every accepted credit must be reviewable, explainable, and defensible.

## 2. Governing Principles

1. GIIS does not promise automatic transfer of every prior credit.
2. GIIS does not copy a prior school's cumulative GPA onto the GIIS transcript.
3. Accepted transfer credits are mapped to the GIIS 24-credit graduation framework.
4. Course-level grades may be converted into the GIIS GPA scale only when the source record is sufficiently reliable.
5. Pass, Credit, Complete, or ungraded work may count for credit but should normally be excluded from GPA.
6. Missing, unclear, altered, or unverifiable records require validation before final credit is granted.
7. Payment should not be requested until the transfer path is clear enough for the family to understand the likely plan.
8. The principal or designated academic reviewer must approve final transfer-credit decisions.

## 3. Legal and Operating Boundary

GIIS is a Florida-registered private school. Florida Department of Education guidance states that private school owners are responsible for their educational programs, including curriculum, student assessment, academic credits, grades, graduation requirements, and student records. Florida Statute 1002.42 also describes private-school student records as including courses taken and records of achievement.

Florida's public-school transfer rule is not a direct mandate for GIIS, but it is a useful conservative reference: high-school transfer credits and grades are generally based on official transcripts, with validation when records are missing or when validation is required. GIIS may use similar validation logic while keeping final decisions under GIIS policy.

This SOP does not claim accreditation, state approval of curriculum, College Board authorization, NCAA acceptance, university admission, scholarship eligibility, or automatic recognition by another institution.

## 4. Roles and Authority

Admissions Intake

- Collects the application, family explanation, prior records, translations, and timeline needs.
- Does not promise final credit count, GPA, grade placement, or graduation date.
- Uses the parent-facing scripts in Appendix D.

Academic Reviewer

- Reviews prior records and maps courses to GIIS subject areas.
- Recommends accepted credit, GPA inclusion, validation requirements, and remaining coursework.

President & Principal

- Approves final transfer-credit decisions.
- Handles edge cases, disputes, acceleration requests, and any request that could affect transcript credibility.

Registrar / Admin Operator

- Enters approved transfer rows into the student record.
- Preserves evidence, reviewer notes, dates, and final decision records.
- Confirms transcript output matches the approved decision.

Advisor

- Builds the student's remaining course plan after the credit decision.
- Monitors first-term validation when required.

## 5. Required Inputs

Minimum intake fields:

- Student full legal name and preferred name.
- Date of birth.
- Current grade level and expected start date.
- Previous school name, location, and school type.
- Parent or guardian contact.
- Family's target graduation timeline.
- Whether official records are available.

Preferred records:

- Official transcript for each completed high-school term.
- Report card if transcript is not yet available.
- Course descriptions or syllabi for unusual, accelerated, international, online, homeschool, or dual-enrollment courses.
- Grading scale from the prior institution.
- Credit-hour or course-duration explanation if the prior system is not a standard U.S. high-school credit system.
- Certified or reliable translation for records not issued in English or Chinese.
- Previous school contact if verification is needed.

Do not complete final transfer-credit approval from screenshots alone unless the principal approves a conditional exception.

## 6. Evidence Levels

Level A - Official and Complete

- Official transcript issued by the prior school or authorized record custodian.
- Includes course names, terms, credits, grades, and school identity.
- Best case for credit acceptance and GPA inclusion.

Level B - Official but Incomplete

- Official record exists but lacks course descriptions, credit units, grading scale, or full term details.
- Credit may be conditionally accepted; GPA inclusion may wait for clarification.

Level C - Unofficial but Plausible

- Parent/student copy, report card, portal export, course list, or email from prior school.
- May support preliminary planning only.
- Final credit requires official confirmation or validation.

Level D - No Reliable Prior Record

- No transcript/report card, or records are inconsistent, unverifiable, or altered.
- Do not grant final transfer credit.
- Use placement, portfolio, assessment, or GIIS coursework.

## 7. Standard Workflow

Step 1 - Intake

Admissions identifies whether the student is a new student, transfer student, homeschool transfer, international transfer, or returning GIIS student with outside coursework.

Step 2 - Record Collection

Admissions requests records before payment. If records are unavailable, admissions explains that only a preliminary path estimate can be offered.

Step 3 - Evidence Classification

The academic reviewer classifies each record as Level A, B, C, or D.

Step 4 - Course Mapping

Each prior course is mapped to one GIIS area:

- English Language Arts
- Mathematics
- Science
- Social Studies
- Physical Education / Health
- Elective / Pathway
- World Language or other college-preparatory elective, if accepted by principal review

Step 5 - Credit Decision

For each prior course, the reviewer chooses one decision:

- Accept for credit and GPA
- Accept for credit only
- Accept conditionally pending validation
- Reject as non-transferable
- Defer pending additional records

Step 6 - GPA Decision

The reviewer applies Section 9 and Appendix B.

Step 7 - Placement and Remaining Plan

The reviewer recommends grade placement, remaining credits, first GIIS courses, and whether validation is required.

Step 8 - Principal Approval

The principal or designated approver signs off before final entry or parent communication.

Step 9 - Family Notice

Admissions sends a written summary:

- Records received.
- Credits accepted.
- Credits excluded or pending.
- GPA treatment.
- Remaining courses.
- Whether the decision is final or conditional.
- Next action.

Step 10 - Record Entry and Audit

The registrar enters approved rows, saves supporting evidence, and checks transcript output.

## 8. Credit Acceptance Rules

Accept for credit when:

- The course is high-school level.
- The subject can be mapped to the GIIS graduation framework.
- The student completed the course.
- The record is official or has been validated.
- The course does not duplicate a GIIS course already credited.

Accept for credit only when:

- The course is completed but has no usable letter/numeric grade.
- The prior record says Pass, Credit, Complete, Satisfactory, or equivalent.
- The source grading scale is unclear and cannot be reliably converted.
- The principal decides that credit is fair but GPA inclusion would overstate precision.

Accept conditionally when:

- The family can provide official records soon.
- The record is plausible but incomplete.
- First-term validation is needed.
- A placement assessment or portfolio review is required.

Reject or defer when:

- The course is below high-school level.
- The course is nonacademic and cannot reasonably support GIIS graduation.
- The course duplicates previously credited work.
- The course was failed, withdrawn, or incomplete.
- The record is unverifiable or appears altered.
- The family asks for backdated records, guaranteed graduation, or unsupported credit.

## 9. GPA Conversion Policy

GIIS uses course-level GPA calculation. It does not import a previous school's cumulative GPA.

For every accepted transfer course, choose one GPA treatment:

GPA Included

- Use when the record has a clear letter grade or a numeric grade with a reliable grading scale.
- Convert the course to the GIIS scale.
- Include the converted value in weighted and unweighted GPA calculations.

Credit Only, GPA Excluded

- Use for Pass/Credit/Complete/Satisfactory records.
- Use when no reliable grading scale exists.
- Use when records are incomplete but credit is accepted by principal review.
- Use when the principal determines that GPA conversion would be misleading.

Not Accepted

- Use for failed, withdrawn, incomplete, duplicated, non-transferable, or unverifiable work.

GIIS GPA scale:

| Letter | Unweighted GPA |
|---|---:|
| A or A+ | 4.0 |
| A- | 3.7 |
| B+ | 3.3 |
| B | 3.0 |
| B- | 2.7 |
| C+ | 2.3 |
| C | 2.0 |
| C- | 1.7 |
| D+ | 1.3 |
| D | 1.0 |
| F | 0.0 |

Weighted GPA:

- Default: same as unweighted.
- Add the GIIS advanced-course weighting only when the accepted course is clearly AP-equivalent or advanced under GIIS policy and the principal approves the designation.
- Do not give weighted boost solely because a prior school used "Honors," "Advanced," or an international label unless the course content and record justify it.
- Public materials should describe AP-related transfer decisions as exam-preparation or advanced-course review unless GIIS has a specific approved transcript designation.

Numeric grades:

- If the prior school provides a grading scale, convert using that scale.
- If the grading scale is missing, request it.
- If the scale cannot be obtained, accept credit only or require validation.
- Do not invent letter-grade cutoffs for foreign or nonstandard grading systems.

## 10. Validation Rules

Validation is required when:

- Official transcript is missing.
- The record is from homeschool, personalized education, informal online study, or a non-school provider.
- The record lacks credit hours, grading scale, or course content.
- Course rigor is unclear.
- The student requests accelerated placement.
- There is a large mismatch between prior grades and current performance.
- The record appears altered, inconsistent, or unverifiable.

Validation methods:

- First GIIS grading-period performance in the next sequential course.
- GIIS placement assessment.
- Portfolio review.
- Course syllabus and work-sample review.
- Written confirmation from prior school.
- Standardized subject assessment, when appropriate.
- Florida-certified teacher recommendation approved by the principal, when appropriate.
- Completion of a GIIS bridge module or diagnostic assignment.

Internal benchmark:

- For high-school validation, a student should generally maintain at least a 2.0 GPA during the first GIIS grading period in the relevant placement.
- If the student does not meet the benchmark, the reviewer must revisit placement and transfer-credit assumptions.

## 12. Transcript Entry Rules

Every accepted transfer row should be entered in a way that remains clear to a future reviewer.

Recommended row elements:

- Course name: use the prior course title when appropriate; add "(Transfer)" only if needed for clarity.
- Course type: Core, Elective, AP Prep, Advanced, or other approved internal type.
- Credits: GIIS accepted credit value, not necessarily the prior school's raw value.
- Grade: converted letter grade if GPA included; blank or "P" if credit only.
- Weighted GPA: computed only when GPA included.
- Unweighted GPA: computed only when GPA included.
- Note in internal record: source school, evidence level, decision, reviewer, date, rationale.

Do not place private reviewer notes, family disputes, immigration details, payment notes, or internal uncertainty on the official transcript.

## 13. Parent-Facing Decision Categories

Use these categories in written family communication:

Accepted Transfer Credit

- The course counts toward GIIS graduation requirements.
- If a usable grade exists, it may affect GPA.

Accepted Credit Only

- The course counts toward graduation credits.
- It does not affect GPA because the grade was not reliably convertible or the record was Pass/Credit only.

Conditional Pending Validation

- GIIS is willing to consider the course, but needs records, translation, first-term performance, portfolio review, or assessment.

Not Accepted

- The course does not count toward GIIS graduation because it is incomplete, below high-school level, duplicated, unverifiable, or outside the GIIS framework.

## 14. Quality Control Checklist

Before sending a final transfer-credit decision:

- Records classified as Level A/B/C/D.
- Student identity matches all records or name change is documented.
- Prior school identity and dates reviewed.
- Each accepted course mapped to a GIIS subject area.
- Credit values checked against the 24-credit framework.
- GPA inclusion decision made course by course.
- Pass/Credit records excluded from GPA unless principal approves another defensible treatment.
- Numeric grades converted only with a reliable grading scale.
- Advanced/AP weighting approved by principal if used.
- Duplicate courses removed.
- Failed/withdrawn/incomplete courses excluded.
- Conditional requirements clearly listed.
- Parent notice avoids guaranteed graduation, college admission, accreditation, AP authorization, NCAA, scholarship, or automatic-transfer claims.
- Principal approval recorded.
- Registrar entry matches the decision summary.

## 15. Record Retention

Save the following:

- Original records received.
- Translation or grading-scale documents.
- Transfer Credit Evaluation Form.
- Parent decision summary.
- Principal approval.
- Any validation evidence.
- Final transcript rows entered.

Store records in the approved student-record location. Do not store sensitive student records in public repo files, public support tickets, screenshots, or unprotected handoff notes.

## 16. Reconsideration and Appeals

A family may request reconsideration if it provides new official records, corrected transcripts, course descriptions, grading scales, translations, or validation evidence.

Reconsideration does not guarantee a different result. The principal makes the final academic decision.

## 17. Red Flags Requiring Principal Review

- Parent asks for a guaranteed diploma timeline before records are reviewed.
- Parent asks GIIS to backdate enrollment, coursework, grades, or graduation.
- Parent asks to copy a previous cumulative GPA without course-level review.
- Records are inconsistent across documents.
- Course completion dates overlap implausibly.
- The student has many credits from informal or unverifiable providers.
- The requested plan would leave too little GIIS learning evidence to defend the transcript.
- Family requests claims involving accreditation, CEEB, AP authorization, NCAA, college admission, or scholarship eligibility.

## Appendix B - GPA Decision Quick Rules

1. Official letter grade: convert to GIIS GPA scale if course is accepted.
2. Official numeric grade with scale: convert through the prior school's scale.
3. Numeric grade without scale: request scale; otherwise credit only or validation.
4. Pass/Credit/Complete/Satisfactory: credit only, GPA excluded.
5. No grade: credit only if completion is defensible; otherwise conditional or reject.
6. Homeschool grades: include only if documentation and validation support the grade.
7. International grades: include only after translation and grading-scale review.
8. Prior cumulative GPA: never copied as GIIS cumulative GPA.
9. Advanced weighting: principal approval required.
10. Doubtful cases: choose credit-only, conditional validation, or escalation.

## Appendix C - Parent-Facing Explanation

Approved short version:

"GIIS reviews transfer credits course by course. Accepted prior coursework may count toward the GIIS 24-credit graduation framework. If an official record includes a reliable letter or numeric grade, GIIS may convert that course grade to the GIIS GPA scale. If the record shows Pass/Credit only, lacks a reliable grading scale, or needs validation, the course may count for credit without affecting GPA. GIIS does not automatically copy a prior school's cumulative GPA, and final transfer-credit decisions require admissions/principal review."

## Appendix D - Family Communication Templates

Pre-review response:

"Thank you for sending the records. We can prepare a preliminary transfer path review, but final credit and GPA treatment require official records and academic review. GIIS reviews prior coursework course by course against our 24-credit graduation framework."

Credit accepted response:

"Based on the records reviewed, GIIS can accept the listed courses toward graduation requirements. Courses with reliable grades will be converted to the GIIS GPA scale; Pass/Credit or non-convertible records will count as credit only and will not affect GPA."

Conditional response:

"Some credits appear potentially transferable, but GIIS needs additional records or validation before final approval. The student may be placed in the next appropriate GIIS course while we confirm whether the prior coursework supports final credit."

Not accepted response:

"GIIS cannot accept the listed course(s) for transfer credit at this time because the records are incomplete, unverifiable, duplicated, below high-school level, or outside the GIIS graduation framework. The student can still complete the needed requirement through GIIS coursework."
